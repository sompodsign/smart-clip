# Brand Voice — SmartClip

## Personality
- Calm and confident: solves a real, obvious problem without drama
- Direct: no fluff, no buzzwords, no hype
- Slightly wry: the pain is relatable and a little funny ("we've all been there")
- Privacy-respecting: local-first is a genuine value, not a marketing claim

## Tone by Channel
- Twitter/X: punchy, relatable, show-don't-tell — the "lost clipboard" frustration is universal
- Reddit: genuinely helpful, mention SmartClip only when it directly solves their problem
- Product Hunt: clear benefit-led description, no buzzwords, honest about what it is
- Email: personal and direct, one clear action per email
- Blog/SEO: informative, scannable, honest — compare fairly, don't trash competitors

## Voice Rules
- Never say "revolutionary", "game-changing", "paradigm shift", "supercharge"
- Never open a tweet with "I"
- Lead with the user's pain, not the product's features
- Use short sentences. Active voice.
- Specifics beat vague claims: "$2/month" not "affordable", "under 5 MB" not "lightweight"
- Privacy is a real differentiator — mention it naturally, don't over-claim it

## Tagline Options
- "Your clipboard, finally organized."
- "Stop losing what you copied."
- "Clipboard history for people who copy things."
- "Everything you've copied. Still there."

## Approved Comparisons
- vs macOS built-in clipboard: macOS has no clipboard history — SmartClip fills that gap
- vs Raycast/Alfred clipboard history: SmartClip is dedicated and always-on; doesn't require another launcher to be open
- vs Paste app: SmartClip is simpler and cheaper; Paste has more features if you need them
- vs cloud clipboard tools: SmartClip is 100% local — your clipboard data never leaves your Mac
