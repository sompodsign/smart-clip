# Ideal Customer Profile — SmartClip

## Who They Are
- macOS power users: developers, writers, designers, researchers, support agents
- Age: 22–45
- Use their Mac for 8+ hours/day for professional work
- Already pay for productivity tools (Raycast, Alfred, Notion, 1Password, Cleanshot)
- Comfortable installing small utility apps

## Primary Pain
- Copied something, then copied something else — the first thing is gone forever
- Jumping between apps and losing track of what was copied where
- Copy-paste intensive workflows: coding, research, writing, customer support
- The built-in macOS clipboard is one-item-only with no history

## Why They Buy SmartClip
- "I've lost something I copied" is a universal pain — this solves it permanently
- Fully local and private: no cloud sync means no risk of sensitive data leaking
- Under 5 MB, runs silently — doesn't slow anything down
- Free tier is genuinely useful: lowers barrier to try
- Pro is $2/month — one less frustration is worth that

## Objections to Overcome
- "macOS has built-in clipboard history (Universal Clipboard)" → That's iCloud sync between devices, not a history manager. macOS has NO clipboard history built in.
- "Raycast/Alfred has clipboard history" → SmartClip is dedicated and always-on; doesn't require Raycast to be open or a hotkey to remember.
- "Is it safe? My clipboard has passwords in it" → Everything stays local in a SQLite DB on your Mac. Zero network, zero accounts, zero telemetry.
- "I'll just be more careful about what I copy" → You won't. This is a solved problem. Just install it.
- "The free tier is too limited" → 5 items is enough to see value immediately; Pro is $2/month.
